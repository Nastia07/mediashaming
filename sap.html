Is fueling War in ukraine

SAP is continuing to support Russian businesses and government-owned organisations as war rages in Ukraine. The German software giant has sold enterprise systems to Sberbank, Aeroflot, and other Russian businesses but, despite the widely condemned invasion of Ukraine, it continues to support such installations. The statement followed specific calls from vice prime minister of Ukraine Mykhailo Fedorov to end SAP's support of Russian organisations as well as software sales.


"This is not enough! Bloody invader's army continues to kill our civil population. We ask you to stop support of SAP products, as long as Russian tanks and missiles attack Ukraine," he said on Twitter. In 2018, SAP said Sberbank had deployed its SuccessFactors HR software to a workforce of 230,000 employees, the largest completed cloud project in Russia, according to the EU's biggest software company. Sberbank is majority-owned by the Russian state. Aeroflot, the leading airline in Russia, said it was deploying a range of SAP systems in 2012, including SAP HANA platform.


SAP has declined to confirm that it has ended support of these specific installations.
